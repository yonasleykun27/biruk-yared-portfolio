WHO I AM

Crafting
Meaningful
Spaces

I am a fifth year architecture student passionate about human centered and problem solving design. I believe architecture should improve people’s everyday experiences by responding to real human needs and spatial challenges. My work is inspired by urban life, public interaction, and the relationship between people and space, which strongly influences my approach to circulation and form development.

Alongside architecture, I have five years of experience in graphic design, working with companies including Weyera and creating T-shirt graphics for religious celebrations and public events in Addis Ababa. This experience has strengthened my creativity and visual communication skills.

01 Residential Apartment

Apartment Design
"Friendly Surveillance"

Inspired by the facade character of 1960s Addis Ababa, this project reimagines the traditional kebele spirit within a modern apartment building. Based on the concept of Friendly Surveillance, it encourages social interaction through shared communal spaces and floor-level gardens, allowing neighbors to connect, cultivate, and support one another while fostering a strong sense of community.

05 High Rise Municipality Design

High Rise Office Tower
"The Civic Hierarchy"

This project presents the design of a high-rise governmental office building developed to accommodate administrative, leadership, and public functions within a unified architectural framework. The central concept is inspired by the idea of civic hierarchy in leadership. Positioned at the upper levels of the tower, leadership spaces are provided with expansive views of the city, symbolizing awareness, responsibility, transparency, and future-oriented decision-making. This concept is translated into the building's massing, circulation, and functional hierarchy, creating a direct relationship between governance and the urban environment.
